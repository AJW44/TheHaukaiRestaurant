OUR STORY


Kia Ora and welcome to The Haukai Restaurant.

Our names are Ania Sophia and Mike Hikau. Throughout our travels over the years we have realised the value of our home land in Aotearoa and the importance of our Maori heritage. Our aim is to provide our customers an experience in te ao Maori with the use of karakia for blessing food and other Maori culture elements.

Food for the Maori symbolizes the freeing of tapu (sacred - spiritual restrictions). It is a time to share sustenance, enjoyment and companionship.

Within Maori tradition, it is important to acknowledge and bless the food, through karakia.

Karakia mō te Kai – Blessing of Food

Traditional Karakia:

Nau mai e ngā hua

o te wao,

o te ngakina,

o te wai tai,

o te wai Māori.

Nā Tane

Nā Rongo

Nā Tangaroa

Nā Maru

Ko Ranginui e tū iho nei

Ko Papatūānuku e takoto nei

Tūturu o whiti kia whakamaua kia tina! TINA!

Hāumi e! Hui e! TĀIKI E!

Welcome the gifts of food

from the sacred forests

from the cultivated gardens

from the sea

from the fresh waters

The food of Tane

of Rongo

of Tangaroa

of Maru

I acknowledge the sky father who is above me,

the earth mother who lies beneath me

Let this be my commitment to all!

Draw together! Affirm!

Haere mai. Come, enjoy, feast. Join our family to experience traditional Maori food and hospitality.
